# Standardized Physical Exam for Snoring/OSA Patients

**Purpose:** Every new snoring/OSA patient at Capital ENT should receive this standardized exam. Results feed directly into the OSA Phenotyper for treatment recommendations.

---

## 1. General Assessment

### Body Mass Index (BMI)
- **How:** Weight (kg) / Height (m)^2. Calculate or use BMI chart.
- **Record:** Exact BMI value (e.g., 31.2)
- **Clinical significance:** BMI ≥30 = major anatomical contributor. BMI ≥35 = poor soft tissue surgery candidate. BMI >40 = HNS exclusion (current FDA/insurance criteria).

### Neck Circumference
- **How:** Measure at the level of the cricothyroid membrane (Adam's apple level) with the patient seated, head in neutral position. Use a flexible tape measure. Record in inches.
- **Landmarks:** Palpate the superior notch of the thyroid cartilage. Wrap tape at this level, snug but not compressing skin.
- **Record:** Inches to nearest 0.5 (e.g., 16.5)
- **Clinical significance:**
  - Men >17" / Women >15" = increased OSA risk
  - HNS staging: Men ≤16" / Women ≤14" = favorable response tier (Ji 2026: 91% response vs. 38% for larger necks)

## 2. Craniofacial Assessment

### Retrognathia
- **How:** With the patient seated, view the face in lateral profile. Assess mandibular position relative to the maxilla.
  1. **Visual assessment:** Does the chin appear recessed? Is there a convex facial profile?
  2. **Clinical test:** Drop an imaginary vertical line from the nasion (bridge of nose). The pogonion (most anterior chin point) should be near or anterior to this line. If the chin falls significantly behind, retrognathia is present.
  3. **Angle's classification:** Check the molar relationship if visible — Class II malocclusion (mandibular molars posterior to maxillary) supports retrognathia.
- **Record:** None / Mild / Moderate-Severe
- **Clinical significance:** Retrognathia independently predicts better MAD response (mandibular advancement directly addresses the deficiency). Also indicates MMA candidacy if surgery is considered.

### Midface Assessment
- **How:** View the patient from the front. Assess maxillary projection — does the midface appear flat or retracted?
- **Record:** Normal / Retracted
- **Clinical significance:** Maxillary and mandibular retrusion together = stronger MAD/MMA indication.

### Micrognathia
- **How:** Assess overall mandibular size. A small mandible (distinct from retrognathia, which is positional) crowds the tongue into a smaller oral cavity.
- **Record:** Present / Absent
- **Clinical significance:** Friedman Stage IV consideration. Strong MMA indication.

---

## 3. Nasal Examination

### External Nasal Valve
- **How:** Inspect the external nose at rest during quiet breathing. Look for alar collapse (inward pulling of the nostrils) on inspiration.
  - **Cottle maneuver:** Place a finger along the lateral nasal wall and gently pull laterally. If breathing improves noticeably, the external valve is contributing to obstruction.
  - **Modified Cottle:** Use a cotton-tipped applicator inside the nostril to stent the valve open.
- **Record:** Normal / Collapse present (unilateral/bilateral)

### Internal Nasal Exam (Anterior Rhinoscopy + Endoscopy)
- **How:** Use a nasal speculum for anterior rhinoscopy, then 0-degree or 30-degree rigid endoscope (or flexible scope) for full evaluation.
- **Assess:**
  1. **Septum:** Deviation present? Grade: mild (<50% obstruction), moderate (50-75%), severe (>75%). Note location (anterior vs posterior, high vs low).
  2. **Inferior turbinates:** Hypertrophied? Grade: mild, moderate, severe. Note mucosal vs bony component. Apply topical decongestant (oxymetazoline) — if turbinate shrinks, it's primarily mucosal. If not, bony hypertrophy.
  3. **Middle turbinates:** Concha bullosa? Paradoxical curvature?
  4. **Nasal polyps:** Present? Grade 1-3.
  5. **Internal nasal valve:** Assess the angle between the upper lateral cartilage and septum (normally ~15 degrees). Narrowing = internal valve stenosis.
- **Record in app:** Nasal obstruction (yes/no), Deviated septum (yes/no), Turbinate hypertrophy (yes/no), NOSE score

### NOSE Score
- **How:** Patient completes the NOSE questionnaire (5 questions, 0-4 scale each, total 0-100).
- **Record:** Score (0-100). Mild <25, Moderate 25-50, Severe >50.

## 4. Oral Cavity Examination

### Friedman Tongue Position (FTP)
- **How:** Patient seated, mouth open wide, tongue RELAXED and resting in the floor of the mouth (NOT protruded — this distinguishes FTP from Mallampati).
  - **FTP I:** Entire uvula and tonsils/pillars visible
  - **FTP II:** Uvula visible but not tonsils/pillars
  - **FTP III:** Soft palate visible, uvula base only
  - **FTP IV:** Only hard palate visible
- **Key distinction from Mallampati:** Mallampati has the patient protrude the tongue. FTP does NOT — tongue stays relaxed. FTP better predicts surgical outcomes.
- **Record:** I, II, III, or IV
- **Clinical significance:** FTP I-II = "low tongue position" = favorable for UPPP. FTP III-IV = "high tongue position" = poor UPPP candidate, consider tongue base surgery, HNS, or MMA.

### Mallampati Score (Modified)
- **How:** Patient seated, mouth open maximally, tongue PROTRUDED as far as possible. No phonation.
  - **Class I:** Soft palate, fauces, uvula, anterior and posterior pillars visible
  - **Class II:** Soft palate, fauces, upper portion of uvula visible
  - **Class III:** Soft palate and base of uvula visible
  - **Class IV:** Hard palate only visible
- **Record:** I, II, III, or IV
- **Clinical significance:** Correlates with intubation difficulty and OSA severity. Less validated than FTP for surgical outcome prediction.

### Tonsil Size (Friedman Grading)
- **How:** Patient mouth open, tongue relaxed (same position as FTP assessment). Evaluate bilateral tonsillar size.
  - **Grade 0:** Prior tonsillectomy (no tonsils)
  - **Grade 1:** Tonsils within the pillars (< 25% of oropharyngeal width)
  - **Grade 2:** Tonsils extending to the pillars (25-50% of width)
  - **Grade 3:** Tonsils extending beyond the pillars (50-75% of width)
  - **Grade 4:** Tonsils touching or "kissing" (>75% of width)
- **Record:** 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4
- **Clinical significance:** Grade 3-4 = strong tonsillectomy indication, especially with FTP I-II (Friedman Stage I → 80% UPPP success).

### Palate Assessment
- **How:** With mouth open, assess:
  1. **Soft palate length:** Longer soft palate = more tissue to vibrate/obstruct. Measure from hard/soft palate junction to uvula tip if possible.
  2. **Palate width:** Narrow palate (high-arched) suggests maxillary constriction.
  3. **Uvula:** Elongated? Thickened? Webbing (low-hanging lateral palate tissue)?
- **Record:** Normal / Elongated palate / High-arched palate / Elongated uvula

### Dental Assessment (for MAD Candidacy)
- **How:** Quick assessment of:
  1. **Tooth count:** Are there enough teeth to retain an oral appliance? Need at least 8-10 healthy teeth per arch (ideally including molars and premolars bilaterally).
  2. **Dental health:** Significant periodontal disease, loose teeth, or extensive caries = MAD risk.
  3. **Dental work:** Full dentures = MAD contraindication. Extensive bridgework/implants = discuss with sleep dentist.
- **Record:** Adequate / Insufficient / Dentures
- **Clinical significance:** Poor dentition = MAD contraindication.

## 5. Jaw Function Assessment (for MAD Candidacy)

### TMJ Assessment
- **How:**
  1. **Palpation:** Place fingers over the TMJ (just anterior to the tragus bilaterally). Have patient open and close mouth. Feel for clicks, crepitus, or tenderness.
  2. **Range of motion:** Maximum opening should be 35-55mm (3 finger-breadths). Limited opening (<35mm) = concern.
  3. **Lateral excursion:** Have patient move jaw side to side. Should be 8-12mm each direction.
  4. **Pain history:** Ask about jaw pain, headaches, clenching, grinding (bruxism).
- **Record:** Normal / Mild dysfunction / Severe dysfunction
- **Clinical significance:** Severe TMJ = MAD contraindication (MAD applies sustained anterior force). Mild TMJ = proceed with caution, monitor closely.

### Mandibular Protrusion
- **How:** Ask patient to slide their lower jaw forward as far as possible (maximum protrusion).
  1. Measure the distance from the upper incisors to the lower incisors at maximum protrusion.
  2. A protrusion range of ≥6mm is generally considered adequate for MAD therapy.
  3. MADs typically start at 50% of maximum protrusion.
- **Record:** mm of maximum protrusion (e.g., 8mm)
- **Clinical significance:** <6mm protrusion = MAD unlikely to be effective. >10mm = good protrusion range.

### Occlusion / Bite Classification
- **How:** Have patient bite down naturally. Assess molar relationship.
  - **Class I (normal):** Lower first molar is half a tooth width ahead of upper first molar
  - **Class II (retrognathic pattern):** Lower molar is posterior to normal position. Subdivided into Division 1 (protruding upper teeth) and Division 2 (retruded upper teeth).
  - **Class III (prognathic):** Lower molar is anterior to normal position
- **Record:** Class I / Class II / Class III
- **Clinical significance:** Class II supports retrognathia diagnosis and predicts better MAD response.

## 6. Pharyngeal Assessment (Flexible Laryngoscopy)

### Mueller Maneuver
- **How:** With flexible nasopharyngoscope in place:
  1. Position scope tip at velopharyngeal level (behind the soft palate)
  2. Ask patient to inhale forcefully with mouth and nose closed (negative pressure breath)
  3. Observe collapse pattern at the retropalatal level
  4. Advance scope to retroglossal level and repeat
  5. Grade collapse: None, Partial (<50%), Significant (50-75%), Complete (>75%)
  6. Note pattern: AP (anterior-posterior), Lateral, Concentric
- **Record:** Collapse degree and pattern at each level
- **Clinical significance:** Predicts (imperfectly) dynamic airway collapse during sleep. Complete concentric retropalatal collapse = HNS contraindication.

### Tongue Base Assessment
- **How:** During flexible laryngoscopy, evaluate:
  1. Lingual tonsil hypertrophy (graded 0-4)
  2. Tongue base contact with posterior pharyngeal wall
  3. Vallecular space depth
- **Record:** Normal / Lingual tonsil hypertrophy / Tongue base crowding

### Epiglottis
- **How:** Visualize epiglottis during scope exam.
  1. Shape: Normal / Omega-shaped (floppy) / Retroflexed
  2. Position relative to tongue base
- **Record:** Normal / Floppy / Retroflexed
- **Clinical significance:** Floppy/retroflexed epiglottis = may cause epiglottic obstruction during sleep → consider epiglottoplasty.

### Hypopharynx/Larynx
- **How:** Standard flexible laryngoscopy to assess:
  1. Supraglottic tissue (arytenoids, aryepiglottic folds)
  2. Vocal cord function
  3. Subglottic assessment if indicated
- **Record:** Normal / Abnormalities noted

## 7. DISE Findings (If Performed)

Drug-Induced Sleep Endoscopy provides dynamic assessment not possible during awake exam. Already captured in the app with existing DISE fields:

### Levels of Obstruction (VOTE Classification)
- **V (Velum/Palate):** Degree (0-2) + Pattern (AP / Lateral / Concentric)
- **O (Oropharyngeal lateral walls):** Degree (0-2) + Pattern
- **T (Tongue base):** Degree (0-2) + Pattern (AP)
- **E (Epiglottis):** Degree (0-2) + Pattern (AP / Lateral)

### Critical DISE Findings for Treatment Selection
- Complete concentric collapse at velum → **Contraindication for HNS** (Inspire)
- Tongue base AP collapse → **Favorable for HNS**
- Lateral wall collapse → **Consider expansion sphincter pharyngoplasty**
- Epiglottic collapse → **Consider epiglottoplasty**

## Friedman Stage (Auto-Calculated)

Derived from FTP + Tonsil Size + BMI:

| Stage | Criteria | UPPP Success |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| I | FTP I-II + Tonsils 3-4 + BMI <40 | ~80% |
| II | FTP I-II + Tonsils 0-2, OR FTP III-IV + Tonsils 3-4 | ~37-74% |
| III | FTP III-IV + Tonsils 0-2 | ~8% |
| IV | Any FTP + BMI ≥40 or significant skeletal deformity | Poor candidate |

## Exam Workflow (Suggested Order)

1. **Vitals/General:** BMI, neck circumference
2. **Face:** Retrognathia assessment (lateral profile), midface assessment
3. **Nose:** External valve → anterior rhinoscopy → endoscopy (septum, turbinates, polyps)
4. **Mouth:** Friedman tongue position → tonsil size → Mallampati → palate → dental assessment → occlusion
5. **Jaw:** TMJ palpation → range of motion → mandibular protrusion measurement
6. **Scope:** Flexible nasopharyngoscopy → Mueller maneuver → tongue base → epiglottis → larynx
7. **DISE** (if indicated, separate visit under sedation)

Total time: ~10-15 minutes for experienced examiner (excluding DISE)
